Our goal

To enable doctors to assess changes in the maxillofacial region as objectively and accurately as possible

To overlay three-dimensional images, we use the voxel method, which is the most accurate. It is based on a very precise movement of one image to another by comparing hundreds of thousands of voxels within a certain reference zone

We don't just connect two images, we break it down into important anatomical elements by segmentation. A neural network helps us in this, which quickly and accurately "pulls out" the necessary anatomical areas from the entire 3D image, be it teeth, jaws or even the temporomandibular joint.Thus, we can evaluate the changes at the level of the teeth, upper or lower jaw separately

Morphometric methods of analysis

The models (before and after) can be divided into a specified number of points and then the distances between the already anatomically equivalent areas can be measured
We can show exactly what spatial and morphometric changes occurred during the growth period over a certain time
